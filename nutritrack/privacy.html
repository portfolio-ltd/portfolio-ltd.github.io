NutriTrack

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 1 June 2026 · Last Updated: 1 June 2026

Introduction

NutriTrack is a gamified nutrition tracking app built for iOS. This Privacy Policy explains what information the app collects, how it is used, and how it is protected.

The app is currently in development and close to release. This policy will be updated as the product progresses toward launch.

Information collection

NutriTrack is designed to minimise data collection and protect user privacy. The app collects only the data required to power the nutrition, hydration, sleep, and gamification experiences.

  • Profile data such as age, height, weight, dietary preferences, and fitness goals are stored locally.
  • Meal logs and custom food items are stored on-device using SwiftData.
  • Authentication data is managed securely with email/password and secure token storage; Sign in with Apple will be added for App Store compliance.
  • HealthKit may be used to sync steps, active energy, water intake, and sleep data when users grant permission.

Use of data

Data is used to:

  • Provide nutrition summaries, meal tracking, and habit progress.
  • Power gamification features like streaks, rewards, and daily missions.
  • Sync optional HealthKit data for hydration and sleep tracking.

We do not sell or share personal information with third parties.

Third-party services

NutriTrack may use Apple services such as HealthKit and App Store authentication. Any data shared with these services is governed by Apple’s privacy policies.

The app does not include analytics or advertising SDKs in the current release-ready build.

Children's privacy

The app is intended for general audience use, and no personal data is knowingly collected from children under 13.

If NutriTrack learns that personal data from a child under 13 has been collected without parental consent, we will take steps to delete it promptly.

Policy updates

We may update this Privacy Policy as the app evolves. When changes are made, the updated policy will be published at this URL with a revised effective date.
